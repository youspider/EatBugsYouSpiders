# EBYS as an Intonation Language Model

EBYS is an ILM where sound is the language being analyzed. Not words, not tokens of text — slices of audio, embedded, attended to, and sequenced in real time. The interface is a DJ deck. The engine underneath is inference.

It doesn't generate from scratch. It remixes what already exists — real recordings from real artists — but selects and combines slices in an order that has never occurred before. Generative not in the sense of synthesis, but of infinite recombination. Every playback is a new arrangement. Nothing repeats.

It's built like a language model. But the language isn't words — it's sound. Emotion. Intonation. The raw, pre-verbal material that music is made of.

Where a large language model learns to predict the next token from context, EBYS learns to select the next slice. The mechanics are the same. The substance is entirely different.

---

## What This Means

A language model builds meaning by stringing tokens together — each word chosen because of what came before, each sentence shaped by the accumulated context of the conversation. Coherence emerges from continuity.

EBYS does the same thing, but the tokens are slices of audio. Each slice carries a descriptor vector — its spectral texture, its energy, its rhythmic position, its emotional color. When EBYS selects the next slice, it's running a similarity search against what just played: finding what fits, what continues, what resolves or complicates the phrase that preceded it.

This is not shuffle. It's not random. It's inference — just operating in a space where the vocabulary is feeling, and grammar is groove.

---

## Language as Sound

Human language evolved to carry meaning — but before syntax, before semantics, there was *intonation*. The rise and fall of a voice. The weight of a pause. The way urgency sounds different from grief even when the words are the same.

Music operates entirely in that pre-linguistic layer. It bypasses the part of the brain that parses sentences and goes straight to the part that feels. EBYS generates in that register. Its "sentences" are tonal arcs. Its "punctuation" is silence and attack. Its "vocabulary" is borrowed from real artists, real performances, real moments captured in Montreal — and recombined into something that has never existed before.

Every generation is a new utterance. None of it repeats.
